PART 2
Amendment of the Nursing and Midwifery Council (Fitness to Practise) Rules 2004
3.—(1) The Nursing and Midwifery Council (Fitness to Practise) Rules 2004 are amended as follows.
(2) After rule 2 (interpretation), insert—

“Meetings and hearings during an emergency situation
2B.  Meetings and hearings arranged under these Rules may be conducted using audio or video conferencing facilities.”.

(3) After rule 5(3) (procedure of the Investigating Committee where the allegation relates to a fraudulent or incorrect entry in the register), insert—

“(3A) In paragraph (3) “venue” includes details of audio or video conferencing arrangements.”.

(4) After rule 11(3) (notice of hearing), insert—

“(3A) In paragraph (3) “venue” includes details of audio or video conferencing arrangements.”.

(5) After rule 19(4) (public and private hearings), add—

“(5) This rule does not apply to hearings conducted by audio or video conferencing.”.

(6) After rule 32(3) (postponements and adjournments), insert—

“(3A) In paragraph (3) “venue” includes details of audio or video conferencing arrangements.”.

(7) In rule 34 (service of documents) F7, for paragraph (1), substitute—

“(1) Any notice of hearing required to be served upon the registrant shall be delivered by sending it by—

(a)a postal service or other delivery service in which delivery or receipt is recorded to, or by leaving it at, the registrant's address in the register; or
(b)a postal service or other delivery service in which delivery or receipt is recorded to, or by leaving it at, where this differs from, and it appears to the Council more likely to reach the registrant at, the registrant's last known address; or
(c)electronic mail to an electronic mail address that the registrant has notified to the Council as an address for communications.”.

EXPLANATORY NOTE
(This note is not part of the Order)

This Order amends the Nursing and Midwifery Council (Fitness to Practise) Rules 2004 (S.I. 2004/1761) (“the Fitness to Practise Rules”), the Nursing and Midwifery Council (Education, Registration and Registration Appeals) Rules 2004 (S.I. 2004/1767) (“the Registration Rules”) and Nursing and Midwifery Council (Practice Committees) (Constitution) Rules 2008 (SI 2008/3148) (“the Practice Committee Constitution Rules”), for the length of an emergency notified under article 9A of the Nursing and Midwifery Order 2001 (S.I. 2002/253).

Part 2 of the Schedule amends the Fitness to Practise Rules. Paragraph 3—

— provides that hearings and meetings may be held via, and that the word “venue” may mean use of, audio or video conference facilities;

— amends the provisions for public and private hearings, to include provision that the requirement to hold hearings in public does not apply to hearings which are held via audio or video conference;

— amends the provisions for the service of documents to allow for electronic service to the registrant's nominated e-mail address.

Part 3 of the Schedule amends the Registration Rules. Paragraph 4—

— provides that hearings may be held via, and that the word “venue” may mean use of, audio or video conference facilities;

— amends the provisions for registrations during the period of the emergency, to allow the Registrar to give an appropriate extension of time for a registrant to renew their registration;

— amends the provisions relating to service of documents in relation to registration appeals, to allow for notices and documents to be sent and received by electronic mail;

— adds a provision to hearing provisions so that, where a hearing is to be held by audio or video conference, there is no obligation to hold it in public.

Part 4 of the Schedule amends the Practice Committee Constitution Rules. Paragraph 5 amends the provisions relating to the constitution of the Practice Committee to allow—

— the Nursing and Midwifery Council to extend the second term of office of a member of a Practice Committee for as long they consider appropriate, and

— for fewer panel members to be present for the committee to be quorate.
